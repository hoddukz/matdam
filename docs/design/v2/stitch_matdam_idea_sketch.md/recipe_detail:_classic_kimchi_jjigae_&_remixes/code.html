Classic
Kimchi Jjigae

By Chef Minji Lee star 4.9 (2.1k reviews)

The soul food of Korea. A rich, spicy, and savory stew made with aged kimchi, pork belly, and tofu. This is the foundational recipe that started the MatDam movement.

Ingredients Serves 2

2 Servings
  • radio_button_unchecked Aged Kimchi
    2 cups
  • radio_button_unchecked Pork Belly
    200g
  • radio_button_unchecked Tofu
    1/2 block
  • radio_button_unchecked
    Gochugaru
    help_outline
    Korean red chili flakes. Provides heat and vibrant red color.
    1 tbsp
  • radio_button_unchecked
    Gochujang
    help_outline
    Korean red chili paste. Savory, sweet, and spicy fermented condiment.
    1/2 tbsp
  • radio_button_unchecked Garlic
    3 cloves
  • radio_button_unchecked Green Onion
    2 stalks

Instructions

1

1 Prep the Ingredients

Prep the Ingredients

Slice the pork belly into bite-sized pieces. Cut the Kimchi into similar sizes (about 1 inch width). Slice tofu into 1/2 inch thick rectangles and chop green onions diagonally.

Pro Tip: Use "Sour Kimchi" (aged kimchi) for deeper flavor. If your kimchi is fresh, add a splash of vinegar.
2

2 Sauté Pork and Kimchi

Sauté Pork and Kimchi

In a pot (preferably a clay pot or ttukbaegi), add the pork belly and cook until some fat renders. Add the kimchi and garlic. Stir fry for about 5-7 minutes until the kimchi turns translucent.

3

3 Add Broth and Simmer

Add Broth and Simmer

Pour in water or rice water (ssaltteumul) to cover ingredients. Stir in Gochugaru and Gochujang. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes to let flavors meld.

4

4 Finish with Tofu & Garnish

Finish with Tofu & Garnish

Add the tofu slices on top. Boil for another 5 minutes. Sprinkle green onions and sesame oil (optional) right before serving. Serve hot with a bowl of steamed rice.
